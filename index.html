The Problem

Hiring is broken because resumes do not measure real ability.

Recruiters spend too much time reviewing profiles, booking interviews, and filtering candidates who were never the right fit. The result is wasted screening time, low signal, and slower hiring decisions.

A

Signal gets buried in volume

Strong candidates can be lost in large applicant pools while weak-fit profiles still make it into interviews.

B

Resumes are easy to optimize

A polished CV can still hide gaps in communication, fit, readiness, or role alignment.

C

Bad interviews are expensive

Every low-quality interview consumes recruiter effort, delays momentum, and raises the cost of a wrong hire.
